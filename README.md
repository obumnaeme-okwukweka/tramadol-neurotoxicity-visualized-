# Tramadol & Neurotoxicity — Visualized
### Exploring the Impact of Tramadol on Vitamin D and Iron Status in the Olfactory Lobe of Wistar Rats

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Tramadol is a widely misused opioid analgesic, particularly among young people in Nigeria and across sub-Saharan Africa. Despite its growing public health burden, data on its neurotoxic effects — especially on micronutrient metabolism in brain regions — remains underexplored.

This project visualizes findings from a controlled 6-week experimental study investigating how tramadol exposure affects **Vitamin D and Iron levels** in the **olfactory lobe** of Wistar rats. The goal is to translate raw laboratory data into clear, accessible visuals that communicate the neurotoxic potential of tramadol misuse.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyse quantitative biochemical and histological data collected over a 6-week study period
- Visualize changes in Vitamin D and Iron concentrations across treatment groups
- Identify statistically significant trends using descriptive and inferential statistics
- Communicate findings in a format accessible to both scientific and public health audiences

---

## 🧪 Study Design

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Animal Model** | Wistar rats |
| **Duration** | 6 weeks (controlled experimental design) |
| **Groups** | Control group vs. Tramadol-exposed groups |
| **Brain Region** | Olfactory lobe |
| **Markers Assessed** | Vitamin D levels, Iron status, Histological changes |
| **Data Collection** | Behavioural monitoring, biochemical assays, tissue processing |

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Methods

- **SPSS** — Descriptive statistics, ANOVA, post-hoc analysis
- **GraphPad Prism** — Scientific data visualization (bar charts, error bars, significance markers)
- **Microsoft Excel** — Data entry, cleaning, and preliminary analysis
- **Histological Techniques** — Tissue processing, staining, microscopy

---

## 📊 Key Findings

- Tramadol-exposed groups showed measurable alterations in Vitamin D and Iron concentrations in the olfactory lobe compared to controls
- Histological analysis revealed structural changes in olfactory lobe tissue with increasing tramadol dosage
- Findings suggest a potential link between tramadol misuse and micronutrient disruption in brain tissue
- Results support growing concern about the neurotoxic effects of long-term tramadol exposure

> ⚠️ *Note: This was an undergraduate research study. Findings are preliminary and intended to contribute to the broader evidence base on tramadol neurotoxicity.*
